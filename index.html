Welcome

This site is hosted to allow for ease in listenting police scanner audio streams. Use of police scanners makes for a neat hobby, however, the startup cost can be great. That is where this site comes in; so sit back, relax, and enjoy!

Currently this site provides access to 2 audio streams as well as links to find more and become involved in this hobby. Check below the streams to see more information. Please SHARE this site!

CNU Audio Stream:

The first audio stream is that of Christopher Newport University (CNU) Police Department; CNU is a small liberal arts college in Newport news, Virgina. The scanner station is located approximately 1 mile from the tower. CNUPD is on the City of Newport News digitally trunked (P25) system.

Groups on this stream: CNUPD Dispatch & CNUPD Special Events

Newport News Audio Stream:

The second stream is that of the City of Newport News; the city is located in southeastern Virginia as a part of the Hampton Roads area. The scanner station is located approximately 8-10 miles from the tower. The city is on the Newport News digitally trunked (P25) system.

Groups on this stream: Fire Dispatch & Fire Tactical (2-6) & Mutual Aid (1-4) & Marine Incident Response Team

The term Police Scanner

"A scanner is a radio receiver that can automatically tune, or scan, two or more discrete frequencies, stopping when it finds a signal on one of them and then continuing to scan other frequencies when the initial transmission ceases.
The terms radio scanner or police scanner generally refer to a communications receiver that is primarily intended for monitoring VHF and UHF landmobile radio systems, as opposed to, for instance, a receiver used to monitor international shortwave transmissions.
More often than not, these scanners can also tune to different types of modulation as well (AM, FM, WFM, etc.). Early scanners were slow, bulky, and expensive. Today, modern microprocessors have enabled scanners to store thousands of channels and monitor hundreds of channels per second. Recent models can follow trunked radio systems and decode APCO-P25 digital transmissions. Both hand held and desktop models are available. Scanners are often used to monitor police, fire and emergency medical services. Radio scanning serves an important role in the fields of journalism and crime investigation, as well as a hobby for many people around the world."
Source: Wikipedia.org

What is a Trunked System?

"A trunked radio system is a complex type of computer-controlled two-way radio system that allows sharing of relatively few radio frequency channels among a large group of users. Instead of assigning, for example, a radio channel to one particular organization at a time, users are instead assigned to a logical grouping, a "talkgroup". When any user in that group wishes to converse with another user in the talkgroup, a vacant radio channel is found automatically by the system and the conversation takes place on that channel. Many unrelated conversations can occur on a channel, making use of the otherwise idle time between conversations. Each radio transceiver contains a microcomputer to control it. A control channel coordinates all the activity of the radios in the system. The control channel computer sends packets of data to enable one talkgroup to talk together, regardless of frequency.
The primary purpose of this type of system is efficiency; many people can carry many conversations over only a few distinct frequencies.[1] Trunking is used by many government entities to provide two-way communication for fire departments, police and other municipal services, who all share spectrum allocated to a city, county, or other entity."
Source: Wikipedia.org
